📞 Call Centre Report Dashboard – 2023

📌 Project Overview

This dashboard was developed to analyze and monitor the operational performance of a call centre for the year 2023. It provides a complete overview of customer interactions, representative performance, revenue generation, customer satisfaction, and regional contribution using interactive Excel visualizations.

The dashboard helps businesses:

Track overall call centre efficiency
Monitor customer satisfaction
Evaluate representative productivity
Analyze branch-wise revenue contribution
Identify operational trends and patterns

🛠 Tools & Technologies Used

Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables
Pivot Charts
Slicers
Conditional Formatting
Data Cleaning & Transformation
Dashboard Designing

📊 Dashboard Features
1️⃣ KPI Cards

The left-side KPI cards provide a quick summary of overall performance:

KPI	Value
Total Calls	850
Total Amount	₹1,97,535
Total Duration	85,312
Average Rating	3.5
Happy Callers	149

These KPIs allow instant monitoring of business performance.

2️⃣ Monthly Call Trend

The Call Trend line chart displays call activity across all months.

Key Observation:
Call volume peaked around May and July
A noticeable decline happened from August to November
Slight recovery observed in December

This helps identify seasonal customer engagement patterns.

3️⃣ Day-wise Call Distribution

The horizontal bar chart shows which weekdays received the most calls.

Insights:
Thursday recorded the highest number of calls.
Tuesday also had high traffic.
Sunday and Wednesday had the lowest engagement.

This insight can help optimize workforce allocation.

4️⃣ Gender-wise Caller Analysis

The stacked column chart compares Female vs Male callers across cities.

Insights:
Kolkata had the highest male caller count.
Mumbai had the highest female caller count.
Female callers were dominant in some cities while male callers dominated others.

This helps understand demographic engagement patterns.

5️⃣ Representative Performance Analysis

The dashboard allows filtering data by representatives (R01–R10).

Key Insights:
R05 handled the highest number of calls.
R03 generated strong performance as well.
R10 recorded the lowest calls handled.

This section helps track employee productivity and efficiency.

6️⃣ Amount Generated by Representatives

The revenue chart shows contribution by each representative.

Insights:
Top-performing representatives contributed significantly more revenue.
Revenue generation does not always directly match call volume, indicating quality of calls also matters.

7️⃣ Customer Rating Analysis

The rating chart displays customer feedback distribution.

Insights:
Most ratings fall between 3 and 4
Fewer customers provided very low ratings
Overall customer satisfaction is moderate and can be improved

8️⃣ Branch-wise Amount Distribution

The pie chart visualizes revenue contribution from different branches.

Insights:
Kolkata contributed the highest revenue share.
Delhi and Bangalore also showed strong contribution.
Revenue distribution is relatively balanced across branches.

🔍 Final Business Insights

✅ Major Findings
📈 Performance Insights
Call traffic was highest during mid-year months.
Thursdays and Tuesdays were the busiest operational days.
Representative R05 emerged as the top performer.

💰 Revenue Insights
Kolkata branch generated the highest revenue contribution.
High call volume does not always guarantee higher revenue.

😊 Customer Satisfaction Insights
Average customer rating of 3.5 indicates acceptable but improvable service quality.
The number of happy callers suggests customer experience initiatives are partially effective.

👥 Demographic Insights
Male and female caller participation varied significantly across cities.
Mumbai and Kolkata showed strong customer engagement.

🎯 Business Recommendations
Improve service quality to increase ratings above 4.
Focus training on low-performing representatives.
Allocate more staff during high-call weekdays.
Analyze top performers’ strategies and replicate across teams.
Increase customer engagement campaigns in lower-performing regions.
